# Glossary

- **ACH**: Automated Clearing House network transfer between financial institutions.
- **Authorized User**: A non-admin user (e.g., bookkeeper) with limited access to assigned entities and entitlements.
- **Available balance**: Balance after accounting for holds and pending items.
- **Current balance**: Balance reflecting posted transactions.
- **Entity / Workspace**: Business customer context for accounts and permissions.
- **E-Sign**: Electronic signature disclosures and consent required for certain electronic transactions.
- **Finicity**: External account aggregation/linking provider used for linking external accounts.
- **Internal transfer**: Movement of funds between accounts within the same entity/workspace.
- **Limit tier**: Bank-defined grouping that governs transaction thresholds based on tenure/standing and risk.
- **MFA / 2-Step Verification**: Multi-factor authentication using an additional factor such as SMS, email, or TOTP.
- **NACHA**: Rules organization governing ACH transactions.
- **Reg E**: Regulation E consumer protections for electronic fund transfers.
- **Same-day ACH**: ACH transfer processed on the same business day subject to additional constraints and limits.
- **TOTP**: Time-based one-time password generated by an authenticator app.
